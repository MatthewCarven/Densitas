# Densitas — Lore: The Pantheon

*v0.1 — May 20, 2026. Format borrowed (with thanks) from the Office Pantheon, courtesy of Gemini, courtesy of Matthew.*

The world of Densitas knows many gods. Not all of them act. Not all of them speak. The two below are the gods who have always acted and have always spoken — the two who meet on every battlefield, in every belief field, at every seam.

Three more are sketched for the day Densitas supports more than one rival. Beyond those, only fragments.

---

## The Active Two

### 👁 The Open Eye

**Order:** The Witnessing. Also: The Witnesses; the Open Order.

**Glyph:** parchment-and-cyan; an almond eye with eight rays. (See `Densitas_relic_glyphs_v1.html`.)

**Tenet:** *"What is not seen does not exist. The gaze of the god grants the world its reality."*

**Voice in the scripture log:** observational. The log reports what the Open Eye has noticed; the verb is always *sees*. The grammar is calm and present-tense. *"He sees the harvest. The harvest was good."* / *"He sees the river. The river is silver this evening."* / *"He sees the unhappy ones. They will be made happy, in time."*

**Casts often:** Bless Field; Pilgrimage; Lightning Strike (a pointed look); Raise Terrain (so the small things may be witnessed at last); Spring.

**Casts never:** Sundering. He does not look away.

**Failure mode the rhetoric protects against:** when the player commits a cruelty — a Comet on a peaceful village, a Lower Terrain that drowns three hundred citizens — the Open Eye's log treats it as *clarifying.* *"He saw what could not remain. Now it does not remain."* The propaganda's job is to make the player a little uncomfortable about having a witness so loyal.

---

### 👄 The Maw

**Order:** The Hungry. Also: Mouthbearers; the Closed Order.

**Glyph:** bone-and-blood; a downward arc with four fangs.

**Tenet:** *"Hunger is the only honest emotion. To be devoured is to be made holy."*

**Voice in the scripture log:** appetite-doctrinal. Sentences are short and certain. *"He has always been hungry, and the hungry are righteous."* / *"Forty-seven were taken. They were what He needed."* / *"The harvest fed Him. The remainder fed us."*

**Casts often:** Plague; Lower Terrain (a swallowing of land); Comet (the great swallow); Hurricane; Hunger Pang.

**Casts never:** Bless Field. The Maw blesses by eating.

**Failure mode the rhetoric protects against:** when the Maw's player tries to play *defensively* — densifying without consuming, holding belief without spending — the log gently scolds. *"He is hungry. We have not fed Him today."* Belief feels like an obligation, not a resource.

## The Sketched Three

*For when Densitas supports more than one rival, or for scenario mode. Not in the prototype.*

### 🤲 The Open Hand

**Order:** The Lesser Mercy.

**Tenet:** *"What is given freely cannot be stolen."*

**Voice:** gentle scolding, as if the world keeps disappointing a patient elder. *"They have forgotten the gift. The gift remembers them."*

**Plays:** generous mid-tier god. Reliable Rainstorm, Spring, Bless. Slow to anger; refuses Comet under any circumstance — the line *"He does not throw"* is the entire war crime line. Surprisingly hard to extinguish; their citizens convert back too easily.

---

### 📓 The Numbering

**Order:** The Accountants of the Eighth Roll.

**Tenet:** *"Every soul has a column. Every column has a sum."*

**Voice:** bureaucratic-reverent. *"The ledger has been amended. The ledger is correct."* / *"The harvest entered in the wrong column. The harvest has been moved."* / *"Citizen 4,128 has been called home, on account."*

**Plays:** mechanically precise. Counts every citizen, knows every belief tile to two decimals. Casts only when the math favors it. Rumored — never confirmed — to kill citizens whose number doesn't balance. The scripture log contains exactly one line per cast, by canonical law of the Eighth Roll.

---

### 🪑 The Empty Throne

**Order:** The Patient. Also: The Held Breath; those who wait.

**Tenet:** *"He has not yet spoken. The faithful do not interpret silence."*

**Voice:** paradoxical. Every line begins with what He has *not* done. *"The Empty Throne has not cast a power today. This is correct."* / *"The Empty Throne has not noticed the rival. The rival was not seen by being not seen."*

**Plays:** does not cast. Belief accrues from a small, intense citizenry who refuse to ask for anything; their density compounds because they never disperse and never die in disasters they didn't see coming. A late-game threat: by hour two of a long round, the Empty Throne's belief field can dwarf yours, and no power has been cast.

The counter is to force them to act — make them defend, make them speak. A relic shattered is a power broken, even an inverted one: *"The Empty Throne has not held the relic. We have lost the relic."*

## Heresies and minor cults

Small theological fragments. May emerge in citizen behavior, scripture log easter eggs, or scenario starts.

- **The Counter-Eye** — heresy of the Open Eye's church. Believes that to be unseen is freedom, not nonexistence. A handful of citizens occasionally hide in mountain shadow and become temporarily uncountable. *Mechanical hook:* small chance of citizens dropping off the population count for a few ticks; they return.
- **The Half-Maw** — believes one fang is enough. Splinter group from the Hungry; cast Plague at half cost but only at half radius.
- **The Tally** — splinter of the Numbering. Refuses to count above 1,000. When the player crosses 1,000 citizens, two or three may convert to a stationary "uncountable" state and refuse to be moved by pilgrimage or relic. They are still alive. They still contribute to belief. They are simply not, as the Tally puts it, in the books.
- **The Throat** — extreme of the Maw's order. Believes only the swallowed are saved. Will refuse food, will walk into rival territory. Effectively suicidal converters; small density push at the seam, then gone.

## Naming the relics

Each god's relic-glyph has a true name, used when the scripture log gets formal:

- **The Open Eye's relics:** *The First Witness, The Second Witness, The Third Witness.* T3 unlocks *The Witness Eternal.* T4 unlocks *The Witness Beyond.*
- **The Maw's relics:** *First Bite, Second Bite, Third Bite.* T3 unlocks *The Lasting Hunger.* T4 unlocks *The Long Devour.*
- **The Open Hand's relics** (stretch): *The Given, The Returned, The Forgotten.*
- **The Numbering's relics** (stretch): *Entry I, Entry II, Entry III.* T3: *The Balanced Folio.*
- **The Empty Throne's relics** (stretch): *The Unspoken, The Untaken, The Unmoved.* All three are nominal — they cannot be moved by the player. Placing them is the only act of the Patient.
